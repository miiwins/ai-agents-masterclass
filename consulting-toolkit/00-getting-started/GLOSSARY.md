# Consulting Toolkit Glossary

## A Quick Reference Guide to Key Terms and Concepts

---

## A

**Agile**
A set of principles and practices for iterative software development and project management that emphasizes flexibility, collaboration, and rapid delivery of value.

**As-Is Process**
The current state of how a process operates today, including all inefficiencies, workarounds, and pain points.

**Assessment**
A systematic evaluation of an organization's current capabilities, maturity, and performance against defined criteria or best practices.

---

## B

**Backlog**
A prioritized list of work items, features, or requirements waiting to be completed (commonly used in Agile/Scrum).

**Baseline**
The starting point measurement used to compare future performance and track improvement.

**Best Practice**
Proven methods or techniques that consistently produce superior results and are widely recognized in the industry.

**BPMN (Business Process Model and Notation)**
A standardized graphical notation for mapping business processes, maintained by the Object Management Group (OMG).

**BPM (Business Process Management)**
The discipline of improving business processes through analysis, design, implementation, monitoring, and optimization.

**Bottleneck**
A point in a process where capacity is limited, causing delays and reducing overall throughput.

---

## C

**Capability Maturity Model (CMM/CMMI)**
A framework that describes the maturity of an organization's processes on a scale from 1 (Initial) to 5 (Optimizing).

**Change Management**
The approach to transitioning individuals, teams, and organizations from a current state to a desired future state.

**CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act)**
California's data privacy law that gives consumers rights over their personal information.

**Continuous Improvement**
An ongoing effort to incrementally improve products, services, or processes over time (also known as Kaizen).

**Current State**
See "As-Is Process"

**Customer Journey Map**
A visual representation of the customer's experience with a product or service across all touchpoints.

**Cycle Time**
The total time from the beginning to the end of a process, including both active work time and waiting time.

---

## D

**Daily Standup (Daily Scrum)**
A short (15-minute) daily meeting where team members synchronize activities and plan for the next 24 hours.

**Data Controller**
Under GDPR, the entity that determines the purposes and means of processing personal data.

**Data Mapping**
The process of documenting what personal data is collected, where it's stored, how it flows, and who has access.

**Data Processor**
Under GDPR, an entity that processes personal data on behalf of the data controller.

**Data Protection Officer (DPO)**
A required role under GDPR responsible for monitoring data protection compliance and serving as a contact point for data subjects.

**DMAIC**
A Six Sigma methodology: Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control - used for process improvement projects.

**DPA (Data Protection Authority)**
Government authority responsible for enforcing data protection regulations in a country/region.

---

## E

**Epic**
A large body of work that can be broken down into smaller stories (used in Agile methodologies).

**End-to-End Process**
A complete process from the customer's perspective, crossing functional and organizational boundaries.

---

## F

**Fishbone Diagram (Ishikawa Diagram)**
A cause-and-effect analysis tool used to identify root causes of problems.

**Five Whys**
A root cause analysis technique that involves asking "Why?" five times to drill down to the fundamental cause of a problem.

**Future State**
See "To-Be Process"

---

## G

**Gap Analysis**
The comparison between current state and desired future state to identify what needs to change.

**GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)**
EU regulation protecting personal data and privacy, effective May 25, 2018.

**Governance**
The framework of rules, practices, and processes by which an organization is directed and controlled.

---

## H

**Handoff**
The point in a process where work is transferred from one person, team, or system to another (often a source of delays and errors).

**Happy Path**
The default scenario in a process where everything goes right without exceptions or errors.

---

## I

**IFRS 17**
International Financial Reporting Standard for insurance contracts, effective January 1, 2023.

**Impact Analysis**
Assessment of the potential consequences of a proposed change.

**Increment**
The sum of all product backlog items completed during a sprint and the value of increments from all previous sprints (Scrum term).

**ITIL (Information Technology Infrastructure Library)**
A framework of best practices for IT service management.

---

## J

**Just-in-Time (JIT)**
A production strategy that strives to reduce flow times and inventory by receiving goods only as they are needed.

---

## K

**Kaizen**
Japanese philosophy of continuous improvement involving all employees from executives to frontline workers.

**Kanban**
A visual system for managing work as it moves through a process, emphasizing continuous flow and limiting work in progress.

**KPI (Key Performance Indicator)**
A measurable value that demonstrates how effectively an organization is achieving key business objectives.

---

## L

**Lead Time**
The time between a customer request and delivery of the product or service.

**Lean**
A methodology focused on maximizing value while minimizing waste, originally from Toyota Production System.

**LeSS (Large-Scale Scrum)**
A framework for scaling Scrum to multiple teams working on one product.

---

## M

**Maturity Model**
A framework for assessing the sophistication and capability of organizational processes, typically on a scale of 1-5.

**Minimum Viable Product (MVP)**
The simplest version of a product that can be released to gather maximum validated learning with the least effort.

**Mudamura-Muri**
Three types of waste in Lean: Mudamura (unevenness), Muda (waste), Muri (overburden).

---

## N

**NAIC (National Association of Insurance Commissioners)**
US organization of insurance regulators from all 50 states and US territories.

**Non-Value Added Activity**
Work that consumes resources but doesn't add value from the customer's perspective.

---

## O

**Optimization**
The process of making something as effective or functional as possible.

---

## P

**Pain Point**
A specific problem that current or prospective customers experience in a process.

**Pareto Principle (80/20 Rule)**
The principle that roughly 80% of effects come from 20% of causes.

**Personal Data**
Under GDPR, any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.

**PII (Personally Identifiable Information)**
Information that can be used to identify, contact, or locate an individual.

**Process**
A series of actions or steps taken to achieve a particular end.

**Process Mining**
A family of techniques using event logs to extract knowledge about actual business processes.

**Process Owner**
The individual responsible for the design, performance, and continuous improvement of a process.

**Product Backlog**
An ordered list of everything that is known to be needed in a product (Scrum artifact).

**Product Owner**
The Scrum role responsible for maximizing product value and managing the product backlog.

---

## Q

**Quality Gate**
A checkpoint in a process where specific criteria must be met before proceeding to the next phase.

**Quick Win**
An improvement that delivers visible value quickly with relatively low effort and cost.

---

## R

**RACI Matrix**
A responsibility assignment matrix defining who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed for each activity.

**Retrospective**
A meeting where teams reflect on what happened and identify improvements for the future (key Agile ceremony).

**Risk**
Potential events or conditions that could negatively impact a project or process.

**ROI (Return on Investment)**
A performance measure used to evaluate the efficiency of an investment: (Gain - Cost) / Cost.

**Root Cause**
The fundamental reason for a problem or defect, rather than just its symptoms.

**Runbook**
Detailed documentation of routine procedures and operations.

---

## S

**SAFe (Scaled Agile Framework)**
A framework for scaling agile practices to large organizations with multiple teams.

**Scope**
The boundaries of a project, defining what is and is not included.

**Scope Creep**
The uncontrolled expansion of project scope without adjustments to time, cost, and resources.

**Scrum**
An agile framework for developing and sustaining complex products, emphasizing teamwork, accountability, and iterative progress.

**Scrum Master**
The servant-leader for the Scrum team, helping everyone understand Scrum theory, practices, and rules.

**Six Sigma**
A set of techniques and tools for process improvement that seeks to reduce variation and defects (3.4 defects per million opportunities).

**SLA (Service Level Agreement)**
A commitment between a service provider and client defining the expected level of service.

**Solvency II**
EU directive codifying and harmonizing insurance regulation across member states.

**SOW (Statement of Work)**
A formal document defining project-specific activities, deliverables, and timeline.

**Sprint**
A time-boxed iteration (typically 1-4 weeks) in Scrum where a potentially shippable product increment is created.

**Stakeholder**
Any person or organization with an interest in or affected by a project or process.

**Standup**
See "Daily Standup"

**Story (User Story)**
A short description of a feature from an end-user perspective, typically following the format: "As a [role], I want [goal] so that [benefit]"

**Story Points**
A unit of measure for estimating the effort required to implement a user story.

**Swimlane**
In process mapping, horizontal or vertical lanes that organize activities by actor, role, or department.

---

## T

**Technical Debt**
The implied cost of additional rework caused by choosing an easy solution now instead of a better approach that would take longer.

**Throughput**
The rate at which a system produces or processes something (e.g., transactions per hour).

**To-Be Process**
The desired future state of a process after improvements have been implemented.

**Touchpoint**
Any interaction between a customer and an organization during the customer journey.

---

## U

**User Acceptance Testing (UAT)**
Testing performed by end users to verify that a solution works for its intended purpose.

**User Story**
See "Story"

---

## V

**Value Stream**
The sequence of activities required to deliver a product or service to a customer.

**Value Stream Mapping**
A Lean technique for analyzing the flow of materials and information through a value stream.

**Velocity**
A measure of the amount of work a team can complete in a sprint (typically measured in story points).

---

## W

**Waste**
Any activity that consumes resources but creates no value for the customer (Seven wastes: Transportation, Inventory, Motion, Waiting, Overproduction, Over-processing, Defects).

**WIP (Work in Progress)**
Work that has been started but not yet completed. Limiting WIP is a key Kanban principle.

**Workflow**
The sequence of processes through which a piece of work passes from initiation to completion.

**Workshop**
A facilitated session where stakeholders collaborate to achieve specific outcomes (e.g., process design, problem-solving).

## Insurance Industry Specific Terms

**Claims Processing**
The workflow from claim submission through investigation, assessment, and payment or denial.

**Loss Ratio**
The ratio of claims paid to premiums earned, a key metric for insurance profitability.

**Underwriting**
The process of evaluating risk and determining pricing for insurance policies.

**Policy Administration**
The systems and processes for managing insurance policies throughout their lifecycle.

**Reinsurance**
Insurance that is purchased by an insurance company to reduce risk.

**Combined Ratio**
Loss ratio plus expense ratio; below 100% indicates underwriting profit.
